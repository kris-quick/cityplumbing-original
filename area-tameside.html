Local Knowledge

Plumbing in Tameside — What You Need to Know

Tameside is one of Greater Manchester's most geographically varied boroughs, running from the relatively flat ground around Droylsden and Audenshaw in the west up into the foothills of the Pennines at Mottram, Longdendale, and Tintwistle. The housing reflects this variety — from the dense Victorian terraces of central Ashton-under-Lyne and Stalybridge to the stone-built cottages of the Pennine edge and the post-war estates that fill Droylsden and Hyde.

Ashton-under-Lyne, in the OL6 and OL7 postcodes, is the main commercial centre of Tameside and has some of the borough's oldest housing stock. The terraced streets around Cavendish Street, Stamford Street, and the areas south towards Denton were built for factory and mill workers and have typical Victorian plumbing characteristics — lead supply pipes in many cases, gravity-fed water systems, and heating that's been added, removed, and changed over many decades. These properties require care and experience to work on, and we bring both.

Hyde (SK14) and Stalybridge (SK15) have a slightly different character. Hyde has a mix of Victorian terraces and inter-war semis, while Stalybridge leans more heavily towards older stone and brick terraces that have some of the most characterful plumbing challenges in the borough. Working in a Stalybridge terrace sometimes means encountering pipe routes that no modern plumber would choose — running through unexpected places, with materials that have been joined together without much regard for consistency. We take these in our stride and work methodically through any issues.

Dukinfield, between Ashton and Hyde, has a significant amount of 1970s housing stock — council-built semis and short terraces that are now at a point where original heating systems are failing or have failed. We carry out a lot of full system replacements in Dukinfield and surrounding streets, and can project manage the whole job from initial survey through to commissioning and handover.
