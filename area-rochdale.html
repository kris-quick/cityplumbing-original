Local Knowledge

Plumbing in Rochdale — What You Need to Know

Rochdale's older housing — the Victorian and Edwardian terraces that fill the OL11 postcode around the town centre, Castleton, and Spotland — was built for mill and factory workers during the height of the town's industrial period. These properties have typically seen multiple generations of partial plumbing updates, with older lead and iron pipework coexisting alongside later copper sections and, in some cases, more recent plastic fittings. Understanding what's behind the walls before starting any job is essential when working in these properties.

Norden and Bamford, on the north side of Rochdale, have a significantly different character. These are predominantly 1960s–1990s semi-detached properties — generally better maintained than the town-centre terraces but with heating systems that are now reaching the end of their intended lifespan. Many of these properties are heated by 1980s and 1990s combination or system boilers that have given good service but are now less efficient and increasingly prone to breakdown. We carry out a lot of boiler replacements in this part of Rochdale and can advise on the best modern alternative for the property size and layout.

Heywood and Milnrow — at the edges of our Rochdale coverage area — have similar characteristics to the main town. The OL12 postcode covers Whitworth and parts of the Pennine fringe, where properties can be older stone-built cottages with non-standard plumbing setups. These properties sometimes present challenges that standard property types don't — unusual pipe routes, non-standard pipe sizes, or older gravity systems that haven't been converted to mains pressure. We take these on and work through them methodically.

For Rochdale landlords, the combination of older housing stock and strong rental demand means gas safety compliance needs to be managed carefully. Certificates must be renewed every twelve months, and any issues identified during inspection need to be addressed before the property can be let. We work with landlords across OL11 and OL12 to keep certificates current and any maintenance issues resolved quickly.
