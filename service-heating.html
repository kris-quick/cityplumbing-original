The Process

How a Central Heating Installation Works

Planned carefully, installed properly, commissioned so it runs correctly from day one.

📋

1. System Survey

We visit the property and assess the space — room sizes, insulation, existing pipework, the boiler (if staying), and what the system needs to achieve. You'll get a full picture before committing to anything.

📐

2. Design

We size the radiators correctly for each room (undersized radiators are a common cause of cold spots), plan the pipework routes and advise on controls — including whether a smart thermostat would suit the setup.

🛠️

3. Installation

Pipework is run, radiators fitted, the system filled and treated with inhibitor to protect against corrosion. We work cleanly and tidy up as we go — no pipes left exposed unless agreed.

✅

4. Commission & Test

The system is commissioned — boiler set up correctly, radiators balanced so each room heats evenly, controls programmed and tested. We walk you through how it all works before we leave.

What to Know

Central Heating in Manchester — Everything You Need to Know

A central heating system is only as good as its design and installation. Oversized radiators waste money. Undersized ones leave rooms cold. Pipework that isn't properly balanced means some rooms overheat while others struggle to reach temperature. These are the kinds of issues that come from cutting corners at the installation stage — and they're also why heating systems installed by experienced engineers perform noticeably better than those fitted by whoever was cheapest on the day.

For new heating installations, we start with a heat loss calculation for each room. This tells us the exact output required from each radiator and helps us specify the right boiler size. An oversized boiler short-cycles (fires up and turns off too quickly), which reduces efficiency and leads to more wear on the components. Getting the boiler size right is one of the most important decisions in a new installation, and it requires proper calculation rather than guesswork.

Power flushing is something a lot of people don't know they need until a heating engineer points it out. Over time, the water in a central heating system picks up rust and sludge — magnetite — from the inside surfaces of the pipes and radiators. This sludge settles in the bottom of radiators (causing cold spots at the bottom of the radiator while the top is hot), in the boiler heat exchanger (reducing efficiency and causing damage), and in the pump (causing it to work harder and eventually fail). The signs you need a power flush include: cold spots in radiators, the system taking a long time to heat up, noisy pipes or boiler, or the system going through frequent boiler breakdowns. Power flushing involves connecting a machine to the system that circulates a powerful cleaning agent at high velocity, dislodging and removing the sludge before refilling with clean water and corrosion inhibitor. It's a significant improvement for an older system and is typically recommended before fitting a new boiler onto an existing pipework circuit.

Smart thermostats have become a popular upgrade for Manchester homeowners in recent years. Systems like Nest, Hive, and Tado replace the standard programmer and thermostat with a connected device you control from your phone. They can learn your routine, adjust to the weather, and let you manage heating room by room if combined with smart thermostatic radiator valves (TRVs). The energy savings depend on your existing habits — if you're already controlling your heating carefully, the gains are modest. But if your heating has been running on a fixed schedule without much thought, a smart system can make a noticeable difference. We fit and configure smart thermostats as part of a heating upgrade or as a standalone job.

Underfloor heating (UFH) is another option we install — both water-based (wet) systems, which are connected to the central heating circuit, and electric systems for smaller areas like bathrooms. Wet UFH is most effective in new builds or major renovations where the floor build-up can be incorporated from the start. It works best with lower system temperatures, which makes it an efficient pairing with a modern condensing boiler or heat pump. We can advise on whether UFH is suitable for your project at the survey stage.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

❓

What is power flushing and do I need it?

Power flushing is a process that cleans the inside of your central heating system — pipes, radiators and heat exchanger — by pushing water and cleaning agent through at high velocity to remove built-up sludge and rust (magnetite). Signs you might need it include cold spots at the bottom of radiators, the system being slow to heat up, noisy pipes, or a boiler that keeps cutting out. If you're fitting a new boiler onto older pipework, most manufacturers recommend power flushing first.

❓

Can you add radiators to an existing system?

Yes. Adding radiators to an existing system is a relatively common job — whether you're converting a loft, adding an extension or heating a room that was previously without. We'll check that the existing boiler has sufficient output to handle the additional load and size the new radiator correctly. In some cases, a system upgrade may be worth considering at the same time.

❓

How long does a central heating installation take?

A full new central heating system in a mid-sized property typically takes 3 to 5 days. Adding radiators or carrying out a power flush is usually a 1-day job. We'll give you a clear timeline at the quote stage based on your specific property and what's involved.

❓

What's the best thermostat for a central heating system?

It depends on your setup and how you want to control it. For straightforward scheduling, a Hive or Nest system is easy to use and well supported. Tado is particularly good if you want room-by-room control combined with smart TRVs. We'll discuss the options with you when we survey the system — there's no one-size-fits-all answer, but there's always a good fit for the way you live.
