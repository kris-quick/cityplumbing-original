Local Knowledge

Plumbing in Wigan — What You Need to Know

Wigan's history as a coal-mining and manufacturing town left it with a legacy of older housing that's still home to many residents today. The terraced streets around Scholes, Newtown, and the town centre were built in the Victorian and Edwardian periods for working families, and they present the same plumbing characteristics as similar housing across Greater Manchester: lead supply pipes in many cases, gravity-fed hot water systems, and heating that's been through multiple generations of updates without ever being fully modernised.

Pemberton, in the WN5 postcode, is an interesting area — it transitions from older terraced housing near the Pemberton boundary to newer family estates towards Orrell and Up Holland. The newer estates have standard modern heating setups, but many of the older Pemberton properties are still running heating systems from the 1970s and 1980s. We carry out a lot of assessment visits in this part of Wigan where homeowners want to understand their options before committing to either repair or upgrade.

Standish is a more affluent part of the Wigan borough, with larger family semis and detached homes that attract owner-occupiers who take pride in their properties. The demand here tends to be for higher-specification bathroom renovations, smart heating controls, and the kind of quality finish that comes from experienced engineers rather than the cheapest available quote. We work in Standish regularly and understand what's expected.

For central heating installations — particularly in properties that have never had gas central heating — Wigan has a reasonable number of these each year. When gas is already connected to a property, converting to full central heating is a straightforward project. When gas supply needs to be extended or a new connection is required, we work alongside the gas supplier and network engineers to coordinate the project from start to finish.
