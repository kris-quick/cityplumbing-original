Local Knowledge

Plumbing in Trafford — What You Need to Know

Trafford is predominantly an owner-occupier borough, and that shapes the kind of plumbing work we carry out here. Homeowners tend to invest in their properties — which means bathroom renovations, boiler upgrades, and central heating improvements are among our most common jobs in this area. The housing stock in Sale and Urmston in particular has aged to the point where many original systems are reaching the end of their practical life, and we're regularly helping families plan and carry out those upgrades.

Old Trafford sits closer to the city and has a denser, older housing profile. The Victorian and Edwardian terraces that make up much of the housing around Shrewsbury Street, Great Stone Road, and Kings Road were built with cast iron and lead pipework, much of which has been partially upgraded but rarely replaced in full. This means you can have sections of original lead supply pipe running into modern plastic connections — which is legal but requires careful handling. We check for lead pipework as a matter of course when we work in these properties.

Stretford has a real mix — some older back-to-backs near the town centre, some 1930s semis, and a few newer developments closer to the Trafford Centre. It's an area where we've done everything from emergency call-outs at 2am to full bathroom renovations that took a week and a half. The M32 postcode covers a lot of ground and we know it well.

Sale, Timperley, and Altrincham edge into our Trafford coverage area and bring with them some of the larger family homes in this part of Greater Manchester. Larger properties often have more complex heating systems — multiple zones, unvented cylinders, or older gravity-fed systems that were the standard in the 1970s. If your heating has quirks or you're not entirely sure how your system works, we can carry out a full assessment and explain what you've got before recommending any work.
